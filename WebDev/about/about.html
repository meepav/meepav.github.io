About Açaí

Name

'Açaí' comes from the Portuguese adaptation of Tupian word [asa'i],
meaning "(fruit that) cries or expels water."

Location

Açaí palms are native to tropical South & Central America along the Amazon
River estuary cultivated on flood plains & swamps

Uses

Used for its fruit (Açaí Berries)

Used for the hearts of palm (Vegetable)

Used for the leaves and trunk wood (Materials & Construction)

Popularity

Its consumption & promotion as a superfood grew in popularity in the mid 1990s,
while global demand for the fruit has expanded rapidly in the 21st century,
and the Açaí palm tree is cultivated for that purpose primarily.
